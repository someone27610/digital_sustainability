Make E-Waste a Thing of the Past

Learn how to recycle, reuse, and reduce electronic waste to protect our planet and future generations.

Learn More Get Started
Toxic Materials

Electronics contain lead and mercury. If dumped, these chemicals harm soil, air, and water.

Health Risks

Unsafe recycling in poorer countries causes serious illnesses, especially in children.

Wasted Resources

Devices have gold, copper, and silver. If not recycled, these valuable materials are lost.

Growing Fast

The world generated 62 million tonnes of e-waste in 2022—and it's increasing rapidly.

Easy & Safe Steps to Recycle Your Devices

Save your data and erase everything with a factory reset or secure wipe to protect your privacy.

Apple, Best Buy, Staples, and phone carriers offer recycling and trade-in options, often for free.

Use certified centers like EcoSpot that safely separate hazardous parts and recover valuable materials.

Give your device a second life—donate to schools or sell on platforms like eBay or Facebook Marketplace.

Look for city-run e-waste collection days for free, safe drop-offs.

Repair and reuse your old gadgets—or turn them into fun DIY projects.
