How Digitalization Supports Sustainability

‚≠ê Tracking & Monitoring

Tracking and monitoring involve using digital technologies to collect and measure real-time data from a business's operation. This is the first and most important step in understanding and managing the environmental impact of a company.

üì° Real-Time Data Collection

Internet of Things (IoT) devices, such as sensors and smart meters, are used to monitor operations live

These devices are used to collect data like:

  • Energy usage (e.g. how much electricity a machine uses per hour)
  • Water consumption (e.g. amount used during manufacturing)
  • Waste production (e.g. quantity of waste generated daily)
  • Air quality and emissions (e.g. carbon dioxide or methane levels)
  • Temperature and humidity (especially in data centres or greenhouses)

üîó Integration with Systems

Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) systems combine data from different departments (supply chain, manufacturing, logistics) in one place.

This integration allows companies to track sustainability performance across the whole operation ‚Äî from raw material sourcing to final product delivery.

üìä Environmental Metrics

Businesses use digital tools to measure key sustainability indicators such as:

  • Carbon Footprint : Total GHG(Greenhouse Gases) emissions from production, transport, and energy use.
  • Energy Efficiency : How much energy is used compared to output (e.g. kWh per product).
  • Water Usage : Total volume of water used and opportunities to reuse or reduce.
  • Material Usage : How much raw material is wasted vs. recycled or reused.

üöÄ Benefits of Monitoring

  • Faster decision-making : Managers can react quickly if energy or emissions go too high.
  • Predictive maintenance : Sensors can warn before machines fail, reducing waste and energy use.
  • Transparency : Businesses can report accurate data to customers, investors, and regulators.
  • Improved compliance : Helps companies follow environmental laws and sustainability goals.

What is a Digital Carbon Footprint?

Digital footprint refers to the environmental impact caused by using digital devices and online services. This includes the greenhouse gas emissions (GHGs) and energy consumption that come from:

  • Making and transporting digital devices (like phones, laptops, TVs)
  • Using the internet, apps, and cloud services
  • Running data centers and networks
  • Disposing of electronic waste (e-waste)
üåç Studies estimate digital emissions account for 1.5% to 4% of total global CO‚ÇÇ ‚Äî about the same as the entire aviation industry.

The digital carbon footprint is important to address because, as the world becomes more digital, internet pollution and carbon emissions from technology continue to rise. If we don‚Äôt take action to reduce it, we may face serious environmental problems in the future.
