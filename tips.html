Practice Your

Digital Sustainability

Discover easy ways to practice digital sustainability and make a real difference through small, mindful changes in your daily digital habits.

Data stored online ‚Äî in your email, cloud, or apps ‚Äî is kept alive by energy-consuming data centers that run 24/7. The more digital clutter we have, the more energy is wasted.

Delete Old Emails

Old emails, especially with attachments, consume server energy. Clean your inbox to reduce unnecessary storage and energy use.

Unsubscribe Unread Emails

Stop receiving newsletters or offers you don‚Äôt read. Reducing email load helps lower server energy usage.

Delete Cloud Clutter

Unused apps and files in the cloud still consume power. Delete what you no longer need to save resources.

Clear Downloads & Trash

A tidy device is more efficient. Clear your downloads and recycle bin regularly to avoid unnecessary data backups.

‚úÖ How it helps: Saves energy, reduces emissions, and extends battery life.

üîå 2. Save Energy While Using Devices

Every time we use a device, it consumes electricity. Using less power means fewer fossil fuels are burned to generate that electricity.

Lower your screen brightness

Bright screens use more power and drain your battery faster. Dimming your screen is a quick way to save energy each day.

Turn off devices instead of using sleep mode

Sleep or standby still uses electricity. Turning devices off completely helps reduce energy waste.

Unplug chargers when not in use

Chargers draw power even when not charging anything. Unplug them to stop this hidden energy use.

Enable battery saver or eco-mode

These modes lower brightness and background activity to save battery and energy automatically.

‚úÖ How it helps: Lowers your digital carbon footprint and improves device performance.

üì° 3. Be Mindful of Internet Use

Streaming and online activity generate massive amounts of data, and that data transfer requires energy.

Stream in lower resolution when HD isn‚Äôt needed

Standard definition (SD) uses much less data and energy. For short or background videos, SD is usually enough.

Download music and videos you play often

Streaming the same content repeatedly uses more energy. Downloading once saves data and reduces energy use.

Avoid endless scrolling on video apps

Autoplay keeps loading new videos, using energy non-stop. Turn it off or set time limits to cut down on waste.

Close unused tabs and apps

Background tabs and apps can constantly refresh and consume data and energy. Closing them helps reduce your digital footprint.

‚úÖ How it helps: Reduces the energy demand of internet infrastructure (data centers, networks).

üì§ 4. Limit Data Storage and Transfer

Transferring and storing digital files ‚Äî especially large ones ‚Äî requires energy for transmission and storage in cloud servers.

Compress large files before uploading or sending

Big files take more time, data, and energy to transfer. Compressing them (like zipping or shrinking PDFs) saves bandwidth and storage space.

Only send attachments when necessary

Skip attachments that aren‚Äôt essential. Fewer large files mean less data to store and less energy used.

Delete old backups and duplicate files

Cloud drives often keep copies you no longer need. Cleaning them out frees up space and lowers energy use.

Use eco-friendly cloud services

Some cloud providers use renewable energy or efficient data centers. Choosing them supports greener tech habits.

‚úÖ How it helps: Decreases data center energy usage and supports cleaner digital habits.
